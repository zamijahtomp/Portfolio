Reflection Introduction

Hello! My name is Zamijah Shakeur-Tompkins and I am a Junior Computer Science major at Howard University from Camden, New Jersey. I serve as the Executive Intern for the Howard University Water Environment Association as well as the Secretary for One of a Kind. In my major, I've taken courses in cloud computing, data analysis, and ultimately learned how to be a better programmer in whatever sector I decide to go into. Throughout my journey, I've found myself trying to use technology for everything, because I thought it was what was expected of me as a CompSci student. But that just isn't the case, and now as I complete Technical Writing, I've found new ways to add writing into my career and every day life. I've gotten better at writing tasks and goals down, documenting my code more descriptively, have learned how to better articulate myself in a professional setting, and overall I learned how to become a more efficient writer.

  2. What are your greatest strengths?
    • My greatest strengths would be my passion for the things I do and my leadership skills. I've successfully led classrooms of children for 6 years, not just teaching subjects but really making sure the scholars understand, and I want them to appreciate their education as much as I do.
  3. What is your greatest weakness?
    • My greatest weakness is my perfectionism. I cannot do a task good enough, I have to know that I succeeded in what I set out to do. At times, this obsession can trap me in a loop of my own doubt, but I always manage to create my greatest work when I feel I haven't done enough as I strive for perfection in all that I do.
  4. Why do you want to work here?
    • After researching WWF's mission and getting a feel for the company's environment after attending headquarters, I'm inspired by the company's dedication to environmental justice and wish to work with such an inspiring company.
  5. Why should we hire you?
    • I look forward to interning with WWF and believe that my skills and experience will make me a great fit for the Knowledge Management position. I believe my cloud computing skills and knowledge in database systems are perfect for this role and want to use my experience as a computer scientist towards the betterment of our society and environment.
  6. Describe a challenge you faced at work and how you dealt with it.
    • As Secretary of One of a Kind, I have to manage event planning and coordination. We had a Valentine's Party coming up, but the venue wasn't secured and there was confusion as to the proper payment method for the reservation. I got in contact with the hosts of the venue and relayed to our Treasurer the correct method of payment, ultimately transferring it to our hosts and securing our spot. I made sure to stay in contact with both the venue hosts and my team members and the party turned out to be a big success.
  7. Where do you see yourself in five years?
    • I see myself managing a cybersecurity team, working together to protect our company's and citizen's data. I would like to come back to my university every now and again to present my role and company to future leaders like me.
  8. How do you handle stress and pressure?
    • I like to take deep breaths and try to look at things from a bird's eye view, and think of an objective solution that results in the least amount of stress. I also drink lots of water, not only to stay hydrated but drinking water is a physical reminder for me to relax and reroute my thinking for a second.
  9. What is your salary expectation?
